PM2.5 Initiative

The SensorWeb team is very passionate about the environment and public health. Therefore, to kick off the SensorWeb project, we are building a crowdsourced PM2.5 air pollution sensor network.

PM2.5 air pollution is a growing problem that affects many parts of the world. Governments around the world are asked to publish PM2.5 index data from their monitoring stations. The problem is that government and private orgazination sensors have limited coverage. As a result, people can not obtain meaningful insights from the data. With the help of people like yourselves, we believe we can fill the gaps in the pollution data to generate high-quality information for the public.

We are looking for volunteers to install PM2.5 sensors on their homes, offices, and schools. Please click the 'Contibute' button if you want to learn more about participating.
